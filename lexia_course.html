What Is Lexia Core5?

📖 ~12 min✦ Quiz required

A Reading Program Built on Science

Lexia Core5 is an adaptive, blended learning program designed to accelerate the development of fundamental literacy skills in PreK–5th grade students. Unlike a workbook or a set of leveled readers, Core5 adjusts to each student's performance in real time — giving them more practice on skills they haven't mastered and moving them forward when they're ready.

The program is built on the Science of Reading — a body of research showing that reading is not a natural process but a skill that must be explicitly taught through systematic, structured phonics instruction. Core5 delivers this instruction through interactive, game-like activities that keep young learners engaged while targeting the underlying skills that make fluent reading possible.

What Core5 Teaches

Phonological Awareness

The ability to hear, identify, and manipulate sounds in spoken language — rhyming, syllables, blending, segmenting.

Phonics & Word Study

The relationship between letters and sounds (phoneme-grapheme correspondence), decoding, and encoding.

Structural Analysis

Prefixes, suffixes, roots, and compound words — building vocabulary through word parts.

Fluency

Reading accurately and automatically so cognitive resources are freed for comprehension.

Vocabulary

Word meanings, context clues, and academic language needed for reading comprehension.

Comprehension

Strategies for understanding, analyzing, and responding to text.

Why Core5 and MBTP Work Together

MBTP is a literature-rich curriculum — it builds comprehension, vocabulary, and love of reading through real books and projects. But it does not teach phonics or decoding. Core5 fills exactly that gap: it provides the structured, systematic phonics instruction that MBTP assumes learners already have.

Think of it this way: Core5 teaches learners HOW to decode words. MBTP gives them great books and ideas to decode those words with. Together, they build a complete reader.

How Core5 Works

📖 ~12 min✦ Quiz required

The Adaptive Engine

When a student opens Core5, the program immediately begins assessing where they are. It starts at their identified level and continuously adjusts — serving harder content when they succeed and looping back when they struggle. No two students follow the same exact path through the program.

Each session is made up of activities that target specific skills in sequence. When a student completes activities in a skill area at or above the success threshold, they advance. If they fall below it, Core5 provides additional practice before moving on.

The Six Skill Areas in Order

1

Phonological Awareness

Hearing and manipulating sounds — rhyme, syllables, phonemes. Foundation of all reading.

2

Phonics & Word Study

Letter-sound relationships, decoding patterns, encoding. The core of explicit reading instruction.

3

Structural Analysis

Word parts — prefixes, suffixes, roots — to build decoding of multi-syllabic words.

4

Fluency

Accuracy and automaticity so reading doesn't require effortful decoding of every word.

5

Vocabulary

Word meanings, context, academic language. Bridges decoding and comprehension.

6

Comprehension

Making meaning from text — inference, main idea, text structure.

Session Length & Frequency

Age GroupRecommended Session LengthFrequency

Key rule: Consistency matters more than session length. A learner who uses Core5 3–5 times per week consistently will progress far more than one who uses it for long stretches infrequently.

What "On Grade Level" Means in Core5

Core5 uses a benchmark system to identify whether a student is on track, slightly behind, or at risk. The program automatically flags students who are falling behind their expected pace. As a guide, your job is to check these flags regularly and use them to inform how you support the learner outside of their Core5 sessions.

Reading the Data

📖 ~12 min✦ Quiz required

Your Most Important Habit: Check the Reports

Core5 generates detailed progress data for every student. As a learning guide, your job is to look at this data regularly — ideally after every session — so you know exactly where each learner is, what they're struggling with, and whether they're on pace.

Key Reports to Know

1

Student Progress Report

Shows which skill areas the student has completed, is working on, and hasn't started. Color-coded: green = on track, yellow = monitor, red = at risk. Check this weekly.

2

Usage Report

Tracks time-on-task. Shows minutes used per day and week. A student who isn't logging enough minutes won't progress regardless of quality. Target: recommended minutes per session × days per week.

3

Skill Alert Report

Flags specific skills where a student is struggling or has stalled. These are your intervention priorities. When you see a skill alert, that skill needs additional support OUTSIDE of Core5 — through direct instruction from you.

4

Benchmark Performance

Compares student performance to grade-level expectations. Shows if the student is on track to meet end-of-year benchmarks. Use this for parent reporting and planning.

The 80% Rule

In the HLPP system, any skill area where a student scores below 80% should be treated as a priority intervention area. This means:

When you see below 80% in a skill area

  • Flag it in your end-of-day planning notes
  • Plan a brief 5–10 minute direct instruction activity on that skill in the next session
  • Use the skill name to search for reinforcement activities (flashcards, games, decodable texts)
  • Document the skill and your plan in the parent report
  • Continue Core5 sessions as normal — the program will also re-target this skill

90–100%MasteredMove forward, celebrate progress with learner
80–89%On TrackContinue current approach, monitor
70–79%MonitorAdd light reinforcement outside Core5
Below 70%Intervention NeededDirect instruction + note in parent report

Your Role as Learning Guide

📖 ~10 min✦ Quiz required

What Core5 Does — And What It Can't

Core5 is exceptionally good at delivering individualized practice, tracking progress, and flagging skill gaps. What it cannot do is notice that a learner is frustrated, tired, or needs a human moment of encouragement. That's your job.

During a Core5 Session

1

Set up the learner and step back

Core5 works best with minimal interruption. Once the student is logged in and working, resist the urge to intervene — let the program do its job.

2

Observe without interfering

Watch for signs of frustration, confusion, or disengagement. If a student has been stuck on the same activity for more than 5 minutes, that's your cue to gently check in.

3

Encourage — don't help answer

If a learner asks you for the answer to a Core5 activity, redirect: "Try your best — the program learns from your answers, even the ones you're not sure about."

4

Note what you observe

Which activities caused visible struggle? Which produced obvious success? These observations supplement the data and belong in your end-of-day notes.

After a Core5 Session

Immediately after the session, check the progress report. Note any skill alerts. Plan one reinforcement activity for the next session targeting the weakest area. Document minutes used.

The guide's most important habit: Pull up the Core5 report every single session — not once a week. Progress data is only useful if you look at it while it's still actionable.

When to Intervene Directly

SituationGuide Response
Student has been on same activity 3+ sessionsProvide direct 5-min phonics instruction on that skill before the next Core5 session
Student score drops below 70% in a skillPause, teach the skill directly, then return to Core5 the next session
Student is frustrated or cryingClose the laptop, take a break, revisit the activity with verbal support first
Student is rushing through activities carelesslyEstablish a pace expectation: "We take our time and think carefully on each one."

Integrating Core5 with MBTP

📖 ~10 min✦ Final quiz

Two Programs, One Complete Reader

Core5 and MBTP address different but complementary aspects of literacy. Understanding how they work together — and how to bridge them — is what makes your instruction cohesive rather than fragmented.

Core5MBTP Language Arts
Phonics, decoding, phonological awarenessComprehension, literary analysis, writing
Adaptive, individual digital practiceGuide-led, literature-based, discussion-driven
Systematic skill progressionConcept-based thematic units
Data-driven, reports every sessionProject-assessed, rubric-based
20–45 min daily45–90 min daily

How to Bridge Them in Practice

1

Schedule Core5 before MBTP Language Arts

Doing decoding practice first warms up the brain for the higher-level reading and analysis work in MBTP.

2

Use Core5 vocabulary in MBTP discussions

When Core5 teaches a new word pattern or root, look for that pattern in the MBTP book the learner is reading. Point it out: "Remember the '-tion' ending from Core5? There it is in your book."

3

Let Core5 struggles inform your MBTP scaffolding

If Core5 flags a learner struggling with vowel teams, pre-teach words with those patterns before the MBTP reading session that week.

4

Document both in your daily log

Your end-of-day record should capture Core5 minutes, skill alerts, and MBTP progress together so you can see the full picture of a learner's literacy development.

The bridge moment: After each Core5 session, spend 2 minutes pointing out one thing the learner just practiced in Core5 that they'll also see in today's MBTP book. This explicit connection accelerates transfer from decoding skill to real reading.
