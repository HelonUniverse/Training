What Is Brightwheel?

📖 ~11 min✦ Quiz required

All-in-One Education Management

Brightwheel is an all-in-one platform that brings together everything a learning program needs to document, communicate, and run smoothly — daily activity logs, family messaging, attendance tracking, photos, learning notes, and more. It is used by thousands of early education programs and micro-schools across the US.

At Helon Universe, Brightwheel is the primary tool for documentation and family communication. Every session you deliver, every observation you make, and every photo you take lives in Brightwheel. Families access their child's updates through the Brightwheel parent app in real time — as the day happens.

What Brightwheel Replaces

Paper Daily Reports

No more handwritten notes at the end of the day. Activities, meals, and observations are logged directly in the app as they happen.

Text Message Updates

No more texting individual parents. All family communication goes through Brightwheel's secure, documented messaging system.

Sign-In Sheets

No clipboards at the door. Digital check-in tracks arrivals, departures, and who is authorized to pick up — with a timestamp.

Scattered Photos

No emailing photos or using personal phones. Photos are taken and shared directly through the Brightwheel app — securely and with parental permission.

Who Uses Brightwheel at Helon Universe

RoleWhat They Use Brightwheel For
Learning GuideLog daily activities, send messages, share photos, mark attendance, document observations after every session
Program DirectorOversee all learner records, review guide activity, manage enrollment, run reports, and monitor family communication
FamiliesView real-time updates on their child's day, receive photos, message the guide, see daily summaries — all from the parent app

Accessing Brightwheel

Brightwheel is available as a mobile app (iOS and Android) and via browser at mybrightwheel.com. You will receive login credentials from your program director. Always use the mobile app during sessions — it is faster and designed for in-the-moment logging. The browser version is best for reviewing detailed reports or completing end-of-day write-ups.

Your job: As a guide, Brightwheel is not optional — it is the official record of your work with each learner. Families depend on it. The program depends on it. Log consistently, log accurately, and log every session.

Daily Reporting & Activities

📖 ~13 min✦ Quiz required

The Daily Report: Your Real-Time Log

The daily report in Brightwheel is the running record of a learner's day. It is visible to families in real time as you add entries. Think of it as a digital version of the pickup conversation — except it's richer, more detailed, and permanently documented.

Families treasure seeing updates appear throughout the day. A photo at 9am, a note about a breakthrough at 10:30, a reading session logged at noon — it brings them into the room when they can't be there.

Activity Types You Will Log

Activity TypeWhat to RecordWhen to Log
📚 Learning / CurriculumProgram used, what was taught, how the learner responded, wins and strugglesDuring or immediately after each Teacher block
📖 ReadingLittleLit session — book, level, time, comprehension notesAfter the LittleLit station ends
💻 Digital LearningLexia Core5 — minutes, level, skills worked on, any alertsAfter each Core5 session
🍎 Meals / SnacksWhat was eaten, how much, appetite notes if relevantDuring or right after meals
📝 Observation / NoteBehavioral moments, emotional state, breakthroughs, concernsAny time — capture moments as they happen
📷 Photo / VideoLearner actively at work — with permission. Show the learning in action.During sessions, at meaningful moments

How to Add an Activity

1

Open the correct learner's profile

Tap the learner's name from your classroom view. Every entry is attached to a specific learner — never log one child's activity on another's profile.

2

Tap "Add Activity" and select the type

The form adjusts to show the right fields for that activity. Choose the type that matches what actually happened — don't use "Observation" as a catch-all for everything.

3

Write a specific, detailed note

"Good session" tells a family nothing. "Completed Level 2 Language Arts — identified all letter sounds correctly, needed one prompt on blending CVC words" tells them everything. Be specific.

4

Post in real time

Posting sends the update immediately to the family. For most activities, post as you go — families appreciate seeing updates unfold throughout the day, not all at once at 3pm.

Quality over speed: One specific, detailed note is worth ten vague ones. Write as if the family is standing next to you, genuinely curious about what their child just did — and why it mattered.

Family Communication

📖 ~12 min✦ Quiz required

Brightwheel Is the Only Channel

All family communication at Helon Universe happens through Brightwheel — not personal text messages, not personal email, not social media. Brightwheel keeps communication documented, professional, and visible to the program director. It also protects your personal contact information.

Three Ways to Communicate with Families

1

Direct Messages

Use the Messages tab to send a private message to a specific family. These go only to that learner's parents or guardians. Use for individual updates, questions, or follow-ups that don't belong in the activity feed.

2

Activity Feed Posts

When you post an activity, it automatically appears in the family's Brightwheel feed. This is not a message — it's more like a news post. Families can react to entries depending on your program's settings.

3

School-Wide Announcements

Your program director sends group announcements to all families through Brightwheel. This is a director function — guides do not send announcements to all families.

Sharing Photos and Videos

Photos are one of the most valued things families receive. A photo of their child engaged in learning connects families to a day they couldn't see.

Do

Take photos that show the learner actively engaged — reading, working on a task, building, writing. Add a short caption describing what they're doing and why it matters to their learning.

Don't

Never share photos without confirmed parental permission. Never photograph other learners without permission from all families. Never use your personal phone camera — use the Brightwheel app only.

Professional Communication Standards

Brightwheel Communication Ground Rules

  • Never share concerns about a learner's behavior or development over Brightwheel without first speaking with your director.
  • Never give families your personal phone number or email. Direct them to Brightwheel messaging — always.
  • Respond to family messages within the same business day whenever possible.
  • If a family raises a concern you can't confidently answer, say "Let me look into that and get back to you" — then loop in your director before responding.
  • Write warmly but professionally. Your director can see every message. Communicate accordingly.

Attendance & Check-In

📖 ~10 min✦ Quiz required

Why Attendance Matters

Attendance in Brightwheel is not just a headcount — it is a legal and operational record. It documents who was in your care, when they arrived, when they left, and who dropped off or picked up. This protects the learner, the family, and Helon Universe. The timestamp is part of the official record — it cannot be edited after the fact.

Check-In at Arrival

1

Mark the learner present immediately

The moment a learner arrives, mark them checked in on Brightwheel. Do not wait — the timestamp is the record. "I'll do it later" creates a gap in the official log.

2

Note who dropped off

Brightwheel lets you record the authorized individual who brought the learner. If someone not on the authorized list drops off, note it and alert your director.

3

Complete any health check

If your program uses Brightwheel's health screening feature at check-in, complete it every time. This is part of your compliance record — not optional.

Check-Out at Departure

1

Confirm the authorized pickup person

Before releasing a learner, check the authorized pickup list in Brightwheel. Do not rely on memory or the person's word — check the app every time.

2

Mark check-out immediately

Record the departure and who picked up as it happens. This closes the attendance record for the day. Do not batch check-outs at the end of your shift.

3

Never release to an unauthorized person

If someone not on the list arrives to pick up, do not release the learner under any circumstances. Contact your director immediately. Trust the list — not the conversation.

Absences

If a learner has not arrived within 15 minutes of their expected start time, mark them absent in Brightwheel and notify your director. Do not assume they are running late — always document and communicate.

Attendance is always real-time: Check in = check in immediately. Check out = check out immediately. The timestamp is part of the official record — batch-entering at the end of the day is not acceptable.

Brightwheel at Helon Universe

📖 ~10 min✦ Final quiz

Two Tools, One Workflow

At Helon Universe, Brightwheel and the Daily Planning tool work as a pair. They serve different audiences and different purposes — but both are required every day.

BrightwheelDaily Planning Tool
AudienceFamilies — real timeYou + the program — internal
What goes inActivities, photos, meals, attendance, messagesDeep session data, program scores, observations, tomorrow's plan
WhenThroughout the day, in real timeEnd of day — analysis and planning
ToneWarm, narrative, family-friendlyProfessional, data-focused, instructional

The Guide's Brightwheel Daily Routine

1

Morning — Check In & Set Up

As each learner arrives, mark check-in immediately. Complete any health check. Open each learner's profile — you'll be switching between them throughout the day.

2

During Sessions — Log as You Go

After each Core5 session → log Digital Learning. After each LittleLit session → log Reading. After each Teacher block → log Learning/Curriculum. Don't batch. Don't wait. Real time, every time.

3

Observations — Capture Them Fresh

Breakthroughs, behavior moments, great quotes, concerns — log an Observation immediately. These details evaporate from memory. If it matters, log it when it happens.

4

Photos — At Least One Per Learner Per Day

Aim for at least one meaningful photo per learner. Not every session — but regularly. Families treasure these. Keep your Brightwheel app camera ready during active work time.

5

End of Day — Check Out & Review

Mark each learner as checked out when they leave. Before closing the app, review each learner's feed: did you log everything? Any family messages to respond to today?

The relationship Brightwheel builds: Families who receive consistent, specific, warm updates from their guide become the most engaged and trusting partners in the learning journey. Your Brightwheel practice IS your relationship-building practice — treat it that way.
